Besser Glossary

IntelliBatch 3.0

The software that controls the conveyor belts leading up to the block machine. Also responsible for the mixers and bins.

Main View Page

The default page of IntelliBatch 3.0 when you first open the application. Where the majority of actions take place.

Material Handling Page

The page that controls the conveyors used to refill material bins.

Delivery Hopper

The hopper that dry aggregate from the quarry is dropped into.

Aggregate Handling Incline Belt

The conveyor that moves dry aggregate into the warehouse onto the shuttle belt.

Shuttle Belt

Located above the bins. The conveyor belt that disposes imported aggregate into the appropriate bin. This belt can be activated in the “Material Handling” page in IntelliBatch 3.0. This belt and be run in both forward and reverse directions.

Bins

The compartments that hold raw material for future batches. In the new Besser plant, there are 5 aggregate bins.

Aggregate Weighbelt

The conveyor that aggregate falls onto from one of the five bins above. Used to measure how much more weight is needed for a complete batch.

Weighbelt Chute

The chute that drops aggregate from the weighbelt into the flexwall belt.

Flexwall Belt

The inclined belt that transports dry aggregate from the weighbelt conveyor into the aggregate hopper for mixing.

Aggregate Holding Hopper

Holds dry aggregate taken out of the bins and is released when the mixing cycle is ready for that batch.

Cement Silos

The silos that holds cement for future batches.

The two silos are actually three silos. The left one is silos #1 and can hold x tons. The right silos is a 50/50 split silos that has two holding containers. These are #2A and #2B and can hold x tons each.

Cement Augers

There are three cement augers coming off of the silos.

Each Cement Auger is a steel tube with a steel auger that transports cement from the silos to the cement hoppers.

Cement Augers

Holds cement taken out of the silos and releases it into the mixer when mixing cycle is ready to start for that batch.

The cement hopper utilizes load cells to weigh up cement for each batch.

Dust Collector

Filters out particulates that are in the mixer during the mixing process.

Mixer

Located up the first flight of stairs. The machine responsible for mixing aggregate, water, cement, and admix together. Can be activated remotely by the “Main View” page of IntelliBatch 3.0 or locally by the control panel next to the mixer.

Water Mixer

Determines how much water goes to the mixer for batching.

Admix Tanks

Holds the admix and then sends through piping when the mixing cycle is calling for it. The left bin is Eopel which makes blocks water resistant, and the right bin is Glenium (plasticizer).

Color Distribution System

Holds bulk bags of granular pigments.

Transports pigment into the mixer for specific color applications in block.

Mud Hoppers

Where mixed material from the mixer drops into.

There are two mud hoppers with hopper #2 used mainly in grey block production. Hopper #1 is used for color blending operations.

Mud Hopper Belt

Where the material in the mud hopper drops onto the machine belt.

Machine Belt

Where material from the mud hopper belt drops onto. Inclines material into the feed belt hopper at the Besser machine.

Feed-belt Hopper

Where material from the incline belt is received. Holds material until the feedbox needs it for producing block.

Besser Machine

The machine that takes the material mixture and forms them into cement blocks.

HMI Control Panel

The control panel that controls actions and operations of the block machine.

Feed-belt

The conveyor belt that distributes material into the feedbox.

Apron Plate

The platform that holds the feedbox.

Feedbox

Comes out and agitates material through the mold for compaction. The feedbox ensures there is enough material evenly distributed for each pallet.

Cut-off Bar

The front gate of the feedbox that has teeth and a wiper that match with the mold when it comes out to pull back excess material.

Tamper Head/Mold Head

The part of the mold that compresses on the material to form a block in the mold and aids in stripping of the blocks. Includes the stripper plate, plungers, and shoes.

Pallet Receiver

Lifts a pallet under the mold for compaction.

Mold

The tool inserted in the machine responsible for forming the block to industry-standard specs.

Assembled Mold Bottom

Holds the material in specific specs for compression. Contains the top frame, core assemblies, division plates, and side core bars.

Machine Belt Disconnect Switch

Powers the machine belt.

Hydraulics Electrical Control Panel

Controls the hydraulic flow and functions of the block machine.

Pallet Delivery System

The part of the machine that receives pallets from the pallet magnet, and delivers them to the pallet receiver.

Front Delivery Conveyor

Conveys pallets from the block machine to the Bessermatic.

Pallet Rejector Controls

Controls the pallet rejector and located on top of the front delivery conveyor.

Bessermatic Control Panel

The control panel that controls actions and operations of the bessermatic.

Bessermatic Loader (Loading side)

Loads pallets onto a rack for delivery into the kiln room.

Rack

A steel shelving unit that holds 108 pallets. Stores 9 pallets high, 4 pallets deep, and 3 pallets wide.

Kiln Room

Kiln Room: An insulated room where wet blocks are stored to be cured.

Cured: Providing adequate moisture, temperature, and time to allow the concrete to achieve the desired properties for its intended use.

Bay

A row of racks in the Kiln. There are 13 bays in the new Besser plant. One bay can hold up to 4 racks.

Car

The unit that takes racks in the kiln to be cured and removed to be palletized. It is also where the crawler is stationed during transport.

Crawler

The unit that loads and unloads racks in either a bay or the bessermatic system.

Bessermatic Unloader (Unloading Side)

Unloads cured blocks onto the unloading conveyor.

Unloading Conveyor

The conveyor that conveys pallets from the unloader to the pallet turnover.

Pallet Scraper Control Panel

The Pallet Scraper Control Panel controls the pallet scraper that clears debris from the pallets before they are stacked to use again. The panel will run on auto as well as allows you to manually control moving the pallets backwards and forwards.

Pallet Scraper

The Pallet scraper removes any debris still on the pallet before it is stacked and used again for more blocks.

Pallet Turnover

Flips pallets 180 degrees to ensure both sides of the pallet are being used for blocks.

Turnover Controls

Controls the rollover either automatically or manually. Located to the right of the rollover.

Pallet Magazine

Where all empty pallets are loaded after going through the Unloading Conveyor. Loaded in queue for the pallet magnet from the bottom up.

Pallet Magazine Controls

Controls the pallet magazine either automatically or manually. Located to the left of the magazine.

Pallet Magnet and Delivery

Uses air pressure and an electromagnet to load pallets into the block machine.

Where the pallet magnet drops off pallets in the block machine. Waits in queue for its turn in cycle.

Besser 2.0 Dryside
Depalleter Panel

The Depalleter Panel controls the Depalleter which unloads the blocks from the pallets and moves then down the platform. This panel controls the:

  • Simultaneous overhead pushers
  • Independent side pushers
  • Turntable
  • Index
Depalleter Turntables

The Depalleter turntables turn the blocks 90° from the position they were when they came off the pallets, to the direction they will be so that they can be aligned in a straight line.

Index Pusher

After the blocks are moved off of the turntable, the Index Pusher pushes the blocks to exit them off of the platform after the indexing and side pusher operation.

Depalleter Dogs Ears

The Depalleter Dogs Ears also known as indexing, push a set of blocks forward so that they can be aligned in the correct orientation to be pushed to the block splitter.

Side Index Pushers

There are two sider pushers on either side of the platform. After the dogs have indexed the blocks correctly, the two pushers on either side will then align the blocks into a straight line.

Online Splitter

The online splitter has a blade that splits the blocks. It splits the blocks right before the block turner.

Turner Clamps

The Turner Clamp turn the blocks that will be on the bottom of the cube.

Offline Splitter

The offline splitter is an alternative option to splitting the blocks. It sits on the side of the conveyer rollers to split the blocks as they come down the rollers.

Splitter/Turnover Panel

The Splitter/turnover panel controls the Inline Splitter and the turnover clamps. It also turns on the hydraulic power unit (HPU), resets the safety curtain, allows programming for the turnover, and aids in cubing of block.

Safety Light Curtain

The Safety Light Curtains.

Hydraulic Tank

Powers the mechanical actions of the splitter and depalleter.

Dryside Conveyer

The dryside conveyer moves the blocks from the Depalleter/splitter/turnover to the patternmaker. The conveyer is made up of powered rollers.

Dryside Patternmaker

The Patternmaker moves the blocks from the conveyer rollers, to the turntables where they can be turned and moved according to the pattern selected before they are moved along to the cuber.

Dryside Panel

The Cuber Panel controls the cuber machine, patternmaker, and the pallet injector programming.

Patternmaker Clamps

Patternmaker clamps allow blocks to move to the shift plates a few at a time to make the pattern of the tiers.

There is a clamp on each side of the block that can be adjusted.

Shift Plate

Part of the patternmaker assembly, the shift plate moves the blocks from the conveyor rollers to the turntables.

Shift Plate Eyes

The shift plate eyes make sure that the blocks are in the proper position and correctly aligned. If blocked, they will stop the operation.

NOTE: The eyes are positioned to read the amount of blocks pushed onto the shift plate. The eyes are set up to read positions of 8”, 16”, and 24”.

Turntable

There are four turntables that move blocks to the correct direction depending on the pattern design. These turntables move in either a clockwise or counterclockwise direction.

Dryside Cuber Machine

The Cuber machine includes:

  • The Cuber Rollers
  • End Stop
  • Side Pushers
  • Tier Pusher
  • Tier Platform
  • Tier Clamps
  • Tier Injector
Cuber Rollers

The Cuber Rollers move the blocks from the turntables into a row so the tier can be made.

End Stop

The End Stop stops blocks at a predetermined point to help make the tier. The End Stop can be moved for 16-inch blocks and 18-inch blocks.

Side Pusher

The Side Pusher pushes the row of blocks forward to assist in creating a tier layer. The side pusher can be programmed in short, medium, and long pushes.

Tier pusher

Once a tier has been made, the Tier Pusher pushes the tier to the lift platform.

Tier Platform

The Tier Platform moves the tier up to the correct level of cube so that the tier can be placed on top of the cube. The platform moves over top of the tier to put the tier on top of the cube.

Tier Clamps

The Tier Clamps move inwards to hold the tier up while the tier platform reverses from under the tier. Once the tier platform has moved backwards to its home position, the clamps disengage the tier, allowing them to drop onto the rollers or the cube being assembled.

Tier Bar

The Tier Bar prevents the block from moving backwards while the Tier Platform reverses from under the tier.

Pallet Ejector

The Pallet Ejector places a wooden pallet onto the rollers to allow tiers to be cubed upon.

Exit Conveyer

The Exit Conveyer is the conveyer that brings the cube made in the cuber to the exit conveyer where it can be picked up and taken to the yard by a forklift.

Cuber Pumps

The pallet pump.

Pallet Dispenser Panel

The Pallet Dispenser Panel controls the pallet injector dispensing the pallets as well as being able to manually move the pallet dispenser and the dispenser forks.

Fortress Key Panel

The Fortress key panel holds the safety keys that allow the machines to run. Without the key, a machine will not run. The keys are interchangeable. To take a key out of the panel, you must take a key from a control panel and put it in the key panel. Then you may remove a key for a guard gate.

Guard Gate

Guard gates encompass some Besser machinery to ensure that workers are safe when working around the equipment. The Guard Gate allows workers to go beyond the guard fencing to work on machines. These gates are interlocked with the machine through a fortress key system.

Fortress Guard Gate Lock

These keyed locks are interlocked with a specific machine. The point of the interlock is to prevent the machines from running while a team member is inside the guarding. Workers can go into the guarded area only if the fortress key has been taken out of the machine and a key has been placed to unlocked the gate.

Vapor Generator

The machine responsible for providing the heat and humidity of the kiln room (inside picture of room on next slide.

Air Compressor

Responsible for providing air pressure for all air drop-downs around the plant, as well as air for all machinery pneumatics.

Control Room

Contains the electrical circuit panels for the HMI monitor on the right and the Bessermatic monitor on the left and the circuit breaker in the middle.

Dryside Catwalk

The catwalks are stairs and a walkway over part of the machine to get from one side of the machine to the other. There is one above the conveyer rollers and one above the Depalleter conveyer.

Mold Rack

The platform that holds molds that are not currently in use.
