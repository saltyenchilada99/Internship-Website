Start-up

Assumptions and Prerequisite Skills
  • The computer is on and IntelliBatch 3.0 is open
  • The proper closing procedures were completed the night before
  • The power switch is on (HMI screen is on)
Supplies Needed
  • Standard PPE
  • Mallet
Step 1

Turn on the lights by door C (Figure 1).

Step 2

To the left of the kiln room door, you will find the air compressor. Push the green button, and check to see if a green checkmark lights up (Figure 2).

Step 3

Right by the air compressor, you will find a door that says “Vapor Generator”. Enter that room and check if the power light (green light) is on. If it isn’t twist and pull out the emergency stop button and press the “reset” button (Figure 3A).

Step 4

Click on the “Run Screen” button. Make sure both rows read “Running” (Figure 3B).

Step 5

If the alarm screen for the generator is showing: Press the “ACK Alarm” to acknowledge the alarm, press “Close” and then press “Reset” (Figure 4).

Step 6

Proceed to the car/platform. For the car, press the “CRM reset” button, followed by the “Pump Start” button, and then the “Auto Start” button (Figure 5A).

Step 7

For the crawler, press the “CRM Reset/Pump Start” button, followed by the “Auto Start” button (Figure 5B).

Step 8

Report to the Bessermatic monitor. Twist and pull out the emergency stop button and press the “CRM Reset” button (Figure 6).

Step 9

Proceed to the Besser HMI. Twist and pull out the emergency stop button and press the “CRM reset" button (Yellow arrow in Figure 7), followed by the “Pump Start Auto” button (Red arrow in Figure 7).

Step 10

Go to the Depalleter. Twist and pull the out the emergency stop button and press the “master start/auto enable” button (Red arrow Figure 8).

Step 11

Proceed to the cuber. Twist and pull out the emergency stop button and press the “Master Start/Auto Enable” button (Red arrow in Figure 9).

Step 12

Go up the first flight of steps and walk straight ahead. Pull up on the disconnect handle (Yellow arrow in Figure 10). Be sure to push up with force.

Step 13

Twist and pull out the emergency stop button and push the “Reset Safety” blue button (Red arrow in Figure 11).

Step 14

Press the “Hydraulic Power On” button (Yellow arrow in Figure 12).

Step 15

Wait about 2 minutes and then hold the switch to the close position (Red arrow in Figure 12) on the “Mixer door 2 open/close” switch until the door/gate is fully closed.

Step 16

Turn the Local/Remote switch to remote (Yellow arrow in Figure 13).

Step 17

Set scaling to manual on the “Main View” page of IntelliBatch (Figure 14).

Step 18

Find the Aggregate Weighbelt icon on the top-right side of the page. Click on the orange triangle in the bottom right corner and click “Run” under reverse. This dumps out any potential debris that hardened up overnight (Yellow circle in Figure 15).

Step 19

Go to the mud hopper. Take out the side screw and lift open the gate flap to break apart any debris that may have stuck overnight (Figure 16A and Figure 16B). Also be sure to remove any stuck debris after the mud hopper as well (Figure 16C). Lastly, remove any debris that may be stuck to the gate in the Machine belt (Figure 16D).

Step 20

Go back to the computer. Set Mud Area to manual and run the Mud Hopper 2 belt by clicking run under the reverse tab (Blue circle in Figure 17). All loose debris will now be in the waste hopper.

Step 21

Click on the value in the Mud Hopper and select “Zero Scale”. This will remove any inaccurate weight measurements on the hopper (Blue circle in Figure 18).

Step 22

For any debris that fell off the mud hopper belt, as well as the debris knocked off from Figure 3D, set the machine belt to reverse. This debris will now be on the floor (Figure 19).

Step 23

Go to the Mud hopper gate and put the bolt back on (Blue circle in Figure 20). NOTE: make sure the mud hopper belt is turned off at this time.

Step 24

Go back to the computer and set mixing to manual (Figure 21).

Step 25

Find the mixer tab, and turn it on by clicking on the orange tringle in the bottom right corner of the icon and holding the start button until you hear the mixer start-up (Blue circle in Figure 22).

Step 26

Click on the button that says “Empty” at the bottom of the screen (Red circle in Figure 23).

Step 27

Click on the “Products” Button (Blue circle in Figure 24).

Step 28

At this point, the “Batch To Block Machine” button will now be available to click on (purple circle in Figure 25).

Step 29

Check and verify that all of the materials are what you wish to produce your first batch with (Red square in Figure 26). If it isn’t, go back and select/deselect any desired bins/silos (See SOPs Selecting/Deselecting Cement Silos and/or Selecting/Deselecting Aggregate Bins).

Step 30

After you are satisfied with all the materials listed in the Ingredient list, Click the “Download” button (blue circle in Figure 26) and click the “Cancel” button (Yellow circle in Figure 26).

Step 31

Set Scaling, Mixing, Discharging, and Mud Area all to auto (Figure 27).

Step 32

Click on the “Pause” button (Yellow circle in Figure 28).

Step 33

Proceed to the HMI screen.

Step 34

Set the Control switch to the Manual position. Press and hold on the “Drop Head Position” until it stops to ensure there is no friction between the head and the mold.

Step 35

Set control to auto, vibrators on, and conveyors on (Figure 29).

Step 36

Make sure you are on the “Run Screen” page of the HMI monitor. On the bottom left corner of the screen there would be a button that says “Run Screen” (Figure 30). If there is a blank space on the bottom left corner of the page, then you are in the correct page.

Step 37

Press on the “Hydraulic Flow Valve” button (Figure 31).

Step 38

Hit “Home & Reset”. This will set the mold and receiving pallet to its proper positions (Figure 32).

Step 39

Click on the “Vibration Setup” button on the bottom menu. Set the vibration time to 4.500 seconds (Figure 33).

Step 40

Check to make sure proper head, feedbox, pallet positions and speeds are set up in correspondence with the current mold. These can be checked through the “Head & Pallet Receiver Setup” (Figure 34A), “Feedbox & Cutoff” (Figure 34B), and “Pall. Deliv Pall. Trans Setup” (Figure 34C) buttons, all found in the bottom section of the monitor.

Step 41

Press and hold “Jog Feed Belt” until the F.B Laser value is above the “Feed Box Full Amount” value to the left of the buttons. This will give our testing pallet enough material to produce the proper output (Figure 34).

Step 42

Press the “Single Cycle” button (Red circle in Figure 35).

Step 43

Press the “Start Machine Cycle” button underneath the monitor (Yellow circle in Figure 36).

Step 44

Wait for the first pallet to come out. Check block height, perform a swipe test, and observe texture. If the pallet meets acceptable standards, press the “Full Auto” button on the “Run Screen” page (Green circle in Figure 37) and hit the “Start Machine Cycle” button again. Else, start troubleshooting the reason behind the bad pallet (SOP troubleshooting bad pallets).

Step 45

After about 5-10 minutes, turn on the “Auto Feed” button (red circle Figure 38). This mechanic automatically adjusts feed time based on block height. Be sure to check the pallets regularly for consistency in quality.
