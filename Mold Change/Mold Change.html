Mold Change

Assumptions and Prerequisite Skills
  • Proper clean-up procedure has been completed (at the very least cleaning the feed-box and mold)
  • Must be able to lift at least 50 pounds
Supplies Needed
  • standard PPE
  • Air line with drill, socket extension, and 1-1/8 inch socket
  • Mallet
  • Crowbar
  • Forklift with certified driver
Step 1

Take the yellow tool (Figure 1) that is usually hanging on the wall to the left of the machine. Twist the caps of the cutoff bar until the slits are vertical (Figure 2).

Step 2

Push down on each cap until the bottom washer sticks out (Figure 3). Once the bottom washer appears, pull it out.

Step 3

Carefully pull out the spring caps for each cutoff bar. Be careful not to drop any of the washers.

Step 4

Lift out the cutoff bar and put it off to the side away from the block machine (Figure 5).

Step 5

Assess the cleanliness of the cutoff bar and knock apart any dry debris that you may have missed from previous clean-ups (Figure 6).

Step 6

Assemble your drill, socket extension, and socket to the air line (Figure 7).

Step 7

Take your crowbar and pry the agitator away from the feedbox wall (Figure 8). This gives the drill room to unscrew the agitator bolts.

Step 8

While doing step 7, take your drill and unscrew the agitator bolts. Figure 9 demonstrates the drill set to the reverse setting.

Step 9

Take the bolts out and put them somewhere safe (Red circle in Figure 10).

Step 10

Remove the top parts of the agitator (Purple circle in Figure 10).

Step 11

Remove the agitator and put it somewhere away from the block machine (Figure 11).

Step 12

Assess the cleanliness of the agitator grid and knock apart any dry debris that you may have missed from previous clean-ups.

Step 13

Go to the HMI monitor and press “Home and Reset” to make sure the feedbox is out of the way for the mold change (Figure 12).

Step 14

Put the crayons on the four magnets of the tamper head (Red circle in Figure 13). If the mold only came with two crayons, put one on the front magnet on the first side, and the other crayon on the back magnet of the other side.

Step 15

Go to the HMI monitor and press on the “Screen Selector” button (Red circle in Figure 14).

Step 16

Press on the “Change Mold” button (Yellow circle in Figure 15).

Step 17

Press the “Move to POS 2” button (Green circle in Figure 16).

Step 18

Figure 17 is what the message in the top blue box should say once you complete step 17.

Step 19

Press the “Next” button (Green circle in Figure 18).

Step 20

Press the “Start (Move Mold Out)” button (Green circle in Figure 19).

Step 21

All molds in the new Besser plant have programmed positions for lowering the heads. Press the “CONTINUE Already Programmed” button (Purple circle in Figure 20).

Step 22

Wait until the mold gets unclamped from the head of the besser machine. Once this happens, press the “Start Machine Cycle” button (Red circle in Figure 21).

Step 23

Your screen should now look like Figure 22.

Step 24

Take the two chains on the left side of the machine and wrap one chain on one side of the mold and the other chain on the other side. If the mold you are taking out is an 8x8x16 mold, wrap one side of each chain once. Make sure the leeway of the chains are equal on both sides (Figure 23).

Step 25

Find a forklift driver and notify the worker to come bring a forklift close to the side of the machine.

Step 26

Use the two buttons of the chain-lifter to carefully raise the mold off the mold table and lower the mold onto the forks of the forklift (Figure 24).

Step 27

Once the mold is safely on the forklift, put the cutoff bar in the assembled mold bottom, and put the agitator on top of the mold (Figure 25).

Step 28

Before the forklift driver puts the mold on the mold rack, notify the driver which mold to retrieve.

Step 29

Once the new mold comes, remove the agitator and cutoff bar, and wrap the chains around the mold similarly to step 19.

Step 30

Using the two buttons of the chain-lifter, carefully lift the new mold and place it on the mold table. Make sure the assembled bottom is sitting behind the lip of the mold table (Figure 26).

Step 31

On the “Change Mold” screen, press the “Change Product” button.

Step 32

On the “Change Product” screen, select whichever dimension represents the new mold that is about to be installed into the machine (Figure 28).

Step 33

Press the “Move Mold In To Machine” button (Green circle in Figure 29).

Step 34

The text in the blue box should now look like Figure 30.

Step 35

Place the new agitator on the feedbox and tighten the bolts (refer to steps 6-8). Make sure you set the screwing direction to clockwise this time (Figure 31).

Step 36

Place the new cutoff-bar on the feedbox. Put the springs with all washers back on to. Push the springs down and twist until each spring rotates 90 degrees (Figure 32).

Step 37

Remove the crayons from the new tamper head and keep them somewhere safe (Figure 33).
